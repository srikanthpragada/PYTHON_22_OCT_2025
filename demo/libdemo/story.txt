Every night, when the village fell silent, little Aanya would sit by her window and talk to the Moon. She told it about her day—how she’d tripped in the mud, how her teacher smiled at her drawing, how she missed her grandmother.

The Moon never spoke back, but it always listened. Some nights it hid behind clouds, shy and pale; other nights it shone bright, as if smiling.

One evening, when Aanya was sad and tearful, she whispered, I wish you could talk to me. 

The next night, a soft silver glow filled her room, and she heard a voice like wind over water:
Little one, I always do—just not in words. When I shine, I say I'm proud. When I fade, I rest, like you must too. 

From then on, Aanya never felt alone. Every night, she looked up and smiled—because she knew the Moon was whispering back
